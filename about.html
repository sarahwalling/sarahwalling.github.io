About Me

I am a first year Master's student in the department of Computer Science at the University of Montana, in Dr. Travis Wheeler's lab.

I grew up in Tucson, Arizona where I developed a love for adventure and the outdoors. After a stint in California, I moved to Missoula and obtained my B.S. in Computer Science from the University of Montana. I started in the Wheeler Lab as an undergraduate researcher, focusing on analysis and discovery of Dual-Coding Exons from the very beginning. After two years as an undergraduate, I continue this research in the Wheeler Lab as a graduate student.

When he doesn't accompany me to the lab, I can often be found outdoors with my Labrador, Cooper. In addition to my academic work, I am an avid camper, hiker, and whitewater kayaker. When it's warm, you can always find us on the water!
